Between\u00a0May 2013 and March 2015 at\u00a0opengov.cat<\/a>\u00a0we daily downloaded a copy of a file published by the autonomous government of\u00a0Generalitat de Catalunya. Its name was Organization Chart, Organization Nodes and People in Charge. They created it and updated it daily on their\u00a0\u00a0Open Data Portal<\/a><\/strong>.<\/p>\n

The file listed about\u00a0eight thousand organization nodes and the names of the people in charge of them. <\/strong>Using this information, comparing each file to the one published the day before, we published their weekly changes on a website\u00a0we called the \u00a0Generalitat seismograph <\/a><\/strong>\u00a0and we automatically updated our \u00a0data visualitzacions of each\u00a0Departament\u00a0,<\/a>\u00a0with which we set up this project.<\/p>\n

For two years, we registered and published daily changes in the structure of the Catalan Government and Public Administration.<\/p>\n

We were expecting great changes last spring and this summer, given the interesting period\u00a0of political change we are immersed in at the moment.<\/p>\n

However, last April, just before the seismograph was selected to participate in the international workshop\u00a0Visualizar15 at\u00a0MediaLab Prado<\/a>\u00a0to be further developed,\u00a0\u00a0the seismograph stopped working<\/strong>: first, there was a brief change of location\u00a0(it was updated on a\u00a0different URL) and then the updating process simply came to a halt.<\/p>\n

Therefore, surprised at this, \u00a0we contacted the Open Data government section to ask what was happening to the file after two years of daily updating. This was their answer on 5 May:<\/p>\n

“As we already told you, we are working to produce and offer the file\u00a0Organization Chart, Organization Nodes and People in Charge regularly again. \u00a0But we don’t know when this will be possible. Therefore, we believe it is appropriate to stop publishing it<\/strong>. We will keep you posted about this when we have more information.”<\/p>\n

Since then, we have sent them a few messages\u00a0via email and twitter but there hasn’t been any satisfactory reply.<\/p>\n

The seismograph allowed us to register the birth of the\u00a0Generalitat’s Transparency Policies Programme<\/strong>, responsible for giving access to public information. It was\u00a0\u00a0officially set up in December 2013<\/a>, under the leadership of former magistrate\u00a0N\u00faria Bassols.<\/p>\n

Our seismograph tool registered the establishment of two Transparency Commissions<\/strong>, one technical, one interdepartmental, in February\u00a02015, more than a year later. Simultaneously, the\u00a0\u00a0Programme changed its name to\u00a0Comissionat<\/em><\/strong><\/a>\u00a0 and the area of Transparency was transferred from the Departament of\u00a0Presidency to the\u00a0Departament of\u00a0Governance.\u00a0<\/strong>\u00a0“This implies loss of importance,” our\u00a0Generalitat sources said.<\/p>\n

As there didn’t seem to be enought people working for\u00a0transparency at the Generalitat, the\u00a0Government also\u00a0appointed Gemma Calvet,<\/a>\u00a0Member of Parliament for Catalonia’s\u00a0Republican Left Esquerra as their\u00a0transparency\u00a0\u00a0consultant\u00a0<\/strong>last March.<\/p>\n

Since last May we are also in touch with their parliamentary group and we have expressed to them our concern about this matter. On \u00a025 June they wrote to us to let us know they were writing to the\u00a0Comissionat and also\u00a0Vicepresidency,<\/strong>\u00a0and an answer is still pending.<\/p>\n

We want the\u00a0Generalitat to publish the Organization Chart file again:<\/p>\n

1- to continue offering citizens information about changes in the \u00a0structure of our\u00a0government.<\/p>\n

2- because the amount of information we are storing can be very useful for future political science research.<\/p>\n

Since the\u00a0“unpublishing” of \u00a0the file, we have been working to create it ourselves by using our limited resources. We have encountered several difficulties which we will refer to in another post soon. Meanwhile, have a good summer!<\/p>\n
